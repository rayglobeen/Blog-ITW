Genesis of Operation Sindoor

On April 22, 2025, a horrifying terror attack in Pahalgam, Jammu & Kashmir, shook the nation. 26 innocent tourists were brutally killed by terrorists linked to The Resistance Front (TRF), a proxy of the Pakistan-based Lashkar-e-Taiba.

This tragedy became the tipping point for the Indian government and 1.4 billion Indians, which led to OPERATION SINDOOR—a bold, strategic response to dismantle terror infrastructure across the border. This operation stands as a testimony to India's commitment to defending its people and sovereignty through precise, calculated, and non-escalatory actions.

The Execution of Operation Sindoor

At 1:44 AM on May 7, 2025, the Indian Armed Forces began coordinated air and artillery strikes targeting 9 verified terror launch pads in Pakistan-occupied Kashmir (PoK). These were eliminated with high precision, sending a powerful signal of deterrence.

The nine terrorist camps targeted were: Shawai Nallah (Muzaffarabad), Muridke, Gulpur (Kotli), Barnala (Bhimber), Abbas (Kotli), Sarjal (Sialkot), Mehmoona Joya (Sialkot), Markaz Taiba (Muridke), and Markaz Subhanallah (Bahawalpur).

In retaliation, Pakistan launched over 400 hostile drones toward Indian territories. However, the layered Indian Air Defence network—including S-400 (Sudarshan Chakra), Akash, and Spyder systems—neutralized the threat with near-perfect efficiency.

In the following 48 hours, India conducted follow-up operations targeting 11 Pakistani air bases to cripple their offensive capability. These strikes paralyzed enemy infrastructure deep inside enemy territory, showcasing India’s capability to wage modern, multi-domain warfare.

On May 10, after India decimated Pakistan’s air bases and, according to credible intelligence, struck deep into its nuclear facilities, a panicked Pakistan scrambled to beg for a ceasefire. With help from the United States, Pakistan's DGMO contacted the Indian DGMO to request an immediate ceasefire.

Conclusion: A Message of Strength

Operation Sindoor redefined India’s doctrine against terror: swift, precise, and resolute. India not only delivered justice for the lives lost in Pahalgam but also set a new global benchmark for counter-terrorism operations.

On May 12, India made it clear to the world: India will NOT tolerate terrorism—at any cost.
